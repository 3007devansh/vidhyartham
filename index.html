School Quality Assessment & Assurance Framework

Elevating Educational Standards

To improve the standards of school education, focus has been shifted on the performance evaluation and improvement of schools. The School Quality Assessment and Assurance Framework has been developed to help monitor identified outcomes at all levels and across all spheres of school education.

SQAAF is a set of standards and best practices as paradigm for attaining individual and institutional excellence. It is a comprehensive tool for educationists, leaders, management and all stakeholders involved in making a significant difference in the lives of children.

A Path to Improvement

SQAAF is not intended to rank schools but rather to establish a baseline of acceptable quality for all assessed institutions. It recognizes the unique mission and characteristics of each school while holding them to a common standard of excellence.

Comprehensive School Assessment

The SQAAF framework evaluates schools across seven critical domains that collectively ensure institutional effectiveness and student success. Each domain represents a vital aspect of school operations and educational delivery.

Curriculum, Pedagogy and Assessment

Primary

Teaching & Learning Excellence

Focuses on curriculum implementation, teaching methodologies, assessment practices, and learning outcomes achievement.

Infrastructure - Adequacy, Functionality and Aesthetics

Essential

Modern Learning Facilities

Evaluates physical infrastructure, safety measures, learning resources, and aesthetic environment of the school.

Human Resources

Critical

Qualified & Motivated Staff

Assesses teacher qualifications, professional development, staff welfare, and human resource management practices.

Inclusive Practices

Important

Education for All

Examines provisions for students with special needs, gender equity, and creating an inclusive learning environment.

Management and Governance

Vital

Effective Administration

Reviews administrative processes, financial management, policy implementation, and governance structures.

Leadership

Key

Visionary Leadership

Evaluates leadership quality, vision implementation, decision-making processes, and organizational culture development.

Beneficiary Satisfaction

Outcome

Happy Stakeholders

Measures satisfaction levels of students, parents, teachers, and other stakeholders with school services.

SQAAF Process: Self-Assessment and External Validation

A comprehensive two-phase approach ensuring continuous quality improvement

Two-Phase Assessment Process

Structured Quality Enhancement

The SQAAF process involves two primary phases that work together to ensure continuous improvement in school quality. This systematic approach helps schools identify strengths, address weaknesses, and maintain high educational standards.

1

Self-Assessment Phase

Internal Evaluation

Internal Reflection and Evaluation

This initial phase enables schools to reflect on their current practices and identify areas for improvement. Schools conduct comprehensive self-evaluation across all seven domains.

  • Comprehensive documentation review
  • Stakeholder feedback collection
  • Performance data analysis
  • Gap identification and planning
2

External Validation Phase

Peer Assessment Team

Independent Verification and Validation

In this phase, an external evaluation by a Peer Assessor Team validates the school's self-assessment, ensuring objectivity and credibility in the quality assurance process.

  • Independent peer review
  • On-site verification visits
  • Stakeholder interviews
  • Final assessment report

Eligibility for SQAAF Process

CBSE Affiliated Schools

Current CBSE Schools

Schools affiliated to CBSE must undergo the process of SQAAF and self-assess themselves on the SQAAF Framework every year on SQAAF Portal.

Aspiring CBSE Schools

Schools aspiring to be affiliated to CBSE can also undertake self-assessment against the framework in an offline mode and prepare themselves while submitting application for affiliation.
